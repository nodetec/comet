# Final Light at Aphelion

#writing/essays #deep space# #stations/life

There is a particular kind of evening that only happens far from the sun, when every practical task is complete and the station windows go from useful to ceremonial.
People gather without announcing that they are gathering.
No one says much.
The light is thin, the machinery keeps its own promises, and for a moment the whole outpost feels less like survival and more like witness.

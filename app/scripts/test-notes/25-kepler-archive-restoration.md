# Kepler Archive Restoration

Restoring old observation archives is mostly a fight against formats no one loves anymore.
The useful lesson is that data rarely disappears all at once. It fades by software layer, by forgotten checksum, by the person who knew which folder was the real one retiring without writing it down.
Today we recovered twenty-two nights of clean transit logs from a damaged mirror node and reattached them to the public catalog.
